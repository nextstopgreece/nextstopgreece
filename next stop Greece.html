Why choose Greece for your studies?

Mediterranean lifestyle, safe cities, a rich cultural heritage – and English-taught programs that lead to respected European degrees.

European degree, Mediterranean life

Study in vibrant cities like Athens, Thessaloniki, Patras, Crete, and Ioannina while enjoying sun, sea, food, and culture. Greece offers a student-friendly environment with living costs that are typically lower than in many Northern or Western European cities.

Weekdays in class, weekends exploring islands, mountains, and ancient sites.

Safe, welcoming, and well-connected

Greek cities are known for their safety, hospitality, and strong sense of community. With easy access to the rest of Europe, you can combine your studies with travel, internships, and international opportunities.

Safe cities EU member state Lower living costs Rich culture & history

What can you study in Greece (in English)?

From Bachelor’s to Master’s degrees, Greece offers an expanding range of English-taught programs in both public and private universities.

Undergraduate programs

English-taught Bachelor’s degrees

A growing number of public and private institutions in Greece now offer full Bachelor’s degrees in English. Typical fields include:

  • Business & Economics
  • Computer Science & Engineering
  • Medicine & Health Sciences
  • Tourism & Hospitality
  • Social Sciences
  • Arts & Humanities

We help you compare options so you can choose what truly fits your goals.

Postgraduate programs

English-taught Master’s & beyond

Greece hosts a wide variety of Master’s programs in English, including:

  • STEM & Data
  • Business & Management
  • Maritime Studies
  • Cultural & Classical Studies
  • Health & Sport Sciences

Small cohorts, project-based learning, and close connections to industry and research.

Public universities

  • Recognised across the EU with strong academic reputation
  • Growing number of Bachelor’s and Master’s in English
  • Highly competitive, especially for certain fields
  • Excellent value, particularly for EU citizens

Private universities & colleges

  • Broad offerings of English-taught UG & PG programs
  • Modern, industry-linked curricula & facilities
  • Smaller classes and strong focus on student support
  • International partnerships, internships, and career services

Requirements

Typical application essentials

While each university has its own rules, most English-taught programs will ask for some combination of:

Frequently Asked Questions

A few of the questions students and parents ask us most often about studying in Greece.

Do I need Greek to start studying?

Many Bachelor’s and Master’s programs are fully English-taught, so you can begin without speaking Greek. However, learning Greek gradually will make your life easier and richer. That’s why Greek lessons are built into our pathway.

Are Greek degrees recognised internationally?

Public universities are recognised throughout the EU, and many private institutions have international partnerships and accreditations. During our consultation, we’ll help you understand recognition in your specific field and target countries.

Can I work while I study?

Many students work part-time in sectors like hospitality, tech, and services, depending on visa rules and workload. We can help you set realistic expectations and understand typical schedules.

How much does NextStop Greece cost?

The cost depends on the level of support you need (language, admissions, exam prep, etc.). In our free consultation, we’ll outline different package options clearly so you can choose what fits your situation and budget.
